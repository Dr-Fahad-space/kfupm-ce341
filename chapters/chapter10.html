Chapter 10

Transportation Planning

Travel Demand Modeling and Forecasting

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, it should be possible to:

  • Remember: define the four-step transportation planning process.
  • Understand: explain trip generation, distribution, mode choice, and assignment models.
  • Apply: calculate trip generation rates and perform basic trip distribution.
  • Analyze: evaluate transportation alternatives using planning models.
Key vocabulary

Trip Generation: estimation of number of trips produced by or attracted to zones.
Trip Distribution: allocation of trips between origin and destination zones.
Mode Choice: selection of transportation mode for trips.
Traffic Assignment: allocation of trips to specific network routes.
Trip Purpose: classification of trips by reason (home-based work, non-home-based, etc.).
Level of Service (LOS): quality of traffic flow on transportation facilities.

10.1 Introduction to Transportation Planning

Transportation planning is the process of defining future policies, goals, and investments for transportation systems. This chapter covers the classic four-step travel demand modeling process.

10.2 Trip Generation

Required Bloom level: Apply

Trip generation estimates the number of trips produced by or attracted to specific land uses. Methods include cross-classification and regression models based on household and employment characteristics.

10.3 Trip Distribution

Required Bloom level: Apply

Trip distribution connects trip productions and attractions between zones. The gravity model is the most common method, relating trip-making to zone attractiveness and travel impedance.

10.4 Mode Choice and Traffic Assignment

Required Bloom level: Understand + Analyze

Mode choice models predict travelers' selection among available modes. Traffic assignment allocates trips to network routes, considering congestion effects through iterative equilibrium methods.

Sample Questions

Q1 — Bloom's Level: Remember

Question: What are the four steps of the transportation planning process?
Answer: Trip Generation, Trip Distribution, Mode Choice, and Traffic Assignment.

Q2 — Bloom's Level: Apply

Question: A residential zone has 500 single-family homes with a trip generation rate of 9.5 trips per household per day. How many daily trips are produced?
Answer: Total trips = 500 × 9.5 = 4,750 trips per day
