Chapter 12

Airport Engineering

Airside and Landside Design

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, it should be possible to:

  • Remember: identify airport components and their functions.
  • Understand: explain aircraft characteristics and design standards.
  • Apply: determine runway and taxiway geometric requirements.
  • Analyze: evaluate airport capacity and delay characteristics.
Key vocabulary

Airside: airport areas where aircraft operate (runways, taxiways, aprons).
Landside: airport facilities for passengers and cargo (terminals, parking, access).
Aircraft Reference Code: classification based on wingspan and approach speed.
Runway Design Code (RDC): determines runway length and width requirements.
Airport Reference Point: designated location defining airport coordinates.
Obstruction Clearance: required clear zones for safe aircraft operations.

12.1 Introduction to Airport Engineering

Airports are complex transportation facilities serving aircraft, passengers, and cargo. This chapter covers the design of airside and landside facilities based on ICAO and FAA standards.

12.2 Aircraft Characteristics

Required Bloom level: Remember + Understand

Aircraft characteristics determine airport design requirements. Key parameters include wingspan, wheel loading, approach speed, and takeoff/landing distances for critical design aircraft.

12.3 Runway and Taxiway Design

Required Bloom level: Apply

Runway design considers orientation (wind analysis), length (performance curves), and geometric standards based on aircraft reference code. Taxiways provide connections between runways and terminals.

12.4 Airport Capacity and Planning

Required Bloom level: Analyze

Airport capacity is determined by runway capacity, gate availability, and terminal processing. Planning considers forecast demand, environmental impacts, and land use compatibility.

Sample Questions

Q1 — Bloom's Level: Remember

Question: What is the difference between airside and landside at an airport?
Answer: Airside includes areas where aircraft operate (runways, taxiways, aprons). Landside includes passenger and cargo facilities (terminals, parking, ground access).

Q2 — Bloom's Level: Apply

Question: An airport has a single runway with an arrival capacity of 30 operations per hour and departure capacity of 25 operations per hour. What is the hourly VFR capacity if arrivals and departures are equal?
Answer: For 50/50 mix, capacity is limited by 2 × min(30, 25) = 50 operations per hour
