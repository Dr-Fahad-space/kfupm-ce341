Chapter 8

Transportation Safety and Road Safety Engineering

Crash Analysis and Countermeasures

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, it should be possible to:

  • Remember: define traffic safety terminology and crash types.
  • Understand: explain factors contributing to traffic crashes.
  • Apply: conduct basic crash data analysis and identify patterns.
  • Analyze: evaluate safety performance and recommend countermeasures.
Key vocabulary

Crash: collision involving at least one motor vehicle in transport.
Fatal Crash: crash resulting in at least one fatality.
Injury Crash: crash resulting in non-fatal injuries.
PDO Crash: property damage only crash with no injuries.
Crash Rate: number of crashes per unit of exposure (vehicles, distance, time).
HSIP: Highway Safety Improvement Program for systematic safety improvements.

8.1 Introduction to Transportation Safety

Road safety is a critical aspect of transportation engineering. This chapter covers the fundamentals of crash analysis, safety metrics, and engineering countermeasures to reduce crash frequency and severity.

8.2 Crash Data Analysis

Required Bloom level: Understand + Apply

Crash data provides the foundation for safety analysis. Types of analysis include frequency analysis, rate analysis, and statistical methods for identifying high-crash locations.

8.3 Factors Contributing to Crashes

Required Bloom level: Understand

Crash causation involves human factors (driver behavior), vehicle factors (design and maintenance), and environmental factors (roadway conditions, weather).

8.4 Safety Countermeasures

Required Bloom level: Apply + Analyze

Engineering countermeasures address specific safety problems. The Haddon Matrix organizes countermeasures by phase (pre-crash, crash, post-crash) and factor (human, vehicle, environment).

Sample Questions

Q1 — Bloom's Level: Remember

Question: What are the three categories of factors contributing to traffic crashes?
Answer: Human factors (driver behavior), vehicle factors (design/maintenance), and environmental factors (roadway conditions, weather).

Q2 — Bloom's Level: Apply

Question: An intersection experienced 12 crashes over 3 years with an entering volume of 15,000 vehicles per day. Calculate the crash rate per million entering vehicles (MEV).
Answer: Total volume = 15,000 × 365 × 3 = 16,425,000 vehicles. Crash rate = (12 / 16.425) = 0.73 crashes per MEV
