Chapter 7

Traffic Control Concepts

Signals, Signs, and Markings

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, it should be possible to:

  • Remember: identify types of traffic control devices and their purposes.
  • Understand: explain the principles of traffic signal timing and phasing.
  • Apply: determine appropriate sign placement and visibility requirements.
  • Analyze: evaluate signalized intersection performance and timing plans.
Key vocabulary

Traffic Signal: a power-operated traffic control device that alternately directs traffic to stop and proceed.
Signal Phase: part of the signal cycle allocated to a specific movement or combination of movements.
Cycle Length: total time to complete one full sequence of signal indications.
Green Time: duration of green indication for a particular phase.
Clearance Interval: yellow plus all-red time for safe clearance of the intersection.
Sign Retroreflectivity: ability of a sign to reflect light back to its source.

7.1 Introduction to Traffic Control

Traffic control devices are essential elements of highway safety and operations. This chapter covers the design, placement, and operation of signs, signals, and pavement markings.

7.2 Traffic Signs

Required Bloom level: Remember + Understand

Traffic signs convey information to road users. Categories include regulatory signs (mandatory actions), warning signs (hazard notification), and guide signs (directional information).

7.3 Traffic Signals

Required Bloom level: Understand + Apply

Traffic signals control right-of-way at intersections. Key concepts include signal phasing, timing plans, coordination, and warrants for signal installation.

7.4 Pavement Markings

Required Bloom level: Understand

Pavement markings supplement signs and signals. Types include longitudinal markings (center lines, edge lines), transverse markings (stop lines, crosswalks), and special markings.

Sample Questions

Q1 — Bloom's Level: Remember

Question: What are the three main categories of traffic signs?
Answer: Regulatory signs (mandatory actions), warning signs (hazard notification), and guide signs (directional/informational).

Q2 — Bloom's Level: Apply

Question: A signal has a cycle length of 90 seconds with three phases having green times of 30s, 25s, and 20s. What is the total lost time per cycle if each phase has 3 seconds of yellow and 2 seconds of all-red?
Answer: Lost time per phase = 3 + 2 = 5s. Total lost time = 3 phases × 5s = 15 seconds
