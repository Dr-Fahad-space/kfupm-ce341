Chapter 9

Pavement Design

Flexible and Rigid Pavement Analysis

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, it should be possible to:

  • Remember: identify pavement types and material properties.
  • Understand: explain pavement distress mechanisms and failure modes.
  • Apply: use AASHTO and other methods for pavement thickness design.
  • Analyze: evaluate pavement structural capacity and remaining life.
Key vocabulary

Flexible Pavement: asphalt pavement that distributes loads through granular layers.
Rigid Pavement: concrete pavement that distributes loads through slab action.
ESAL: Equivalent Single Axle Load, standard 18,000 lb axle load for design.
SN: Structural Number representing overall pavement strength.
Modulus of Subgrade Reaction (k): measure of subgrade support for rigid pavements.
Resilient Modulus (Mr): measure of material stiffness under repeated loading.

9.1 Introduction to Pavement Design

Pavements provide a durable riding surface for vehicles. This chapter covers the principles of flexible and rigid pavement design, including material characterization, traffic analysis, and structural design methods.

9.2 Flexible Pavement Design

Required Bloom level: Apply

Flexible pavements use asphalt surfaces with granular base and subbase layers. The AASHTO design method uses structural number (SN) to represent required pavement strength based on traffic and subgrade support.

9.3 Rigid Pavement Design

Required Bloom level: Apply

Rigid pavements use Portland cement concrete slabs. Design considerations include slab thickness, joint spacing, load transfer, and subbase/subgrade support characterized by modulus of subgrade reaction (k).

9.4 Pavement Distress and Performance

Required Bloom level: Understand + Analyze

Pavement distress includes cracking, rutting, and roughness. Performance prediction models estimate pavement life based on traffic loading, environmental factors, and material properties.

Sample Questions

Q1 — Bloom's Level: Remember

Question: What is the difference between flexible and rigid pavement?
Answer: Flexible pavement uses asphalt and distributes loads through granular layers. Rigid pavement uses concrete slabs that distribute loads through slab action and bending stiffness.

Q2 — Bloom's Level: Apply

Question: A pavement section has 4 inches of asphalt concrete (a1=0.44), 8 inches of crushed stone base (a2=0.14), and 10 inches of gravel subbase (a3=0.11). Calculate the structural number (SN).
Answer: SN = (0.44 × 4) + (0.14 × 8) + (0.11 × 10) = 1.76 + 1.12 + 1.10 = 3.98
