Chapter 11

Public Mass Transportation

Transit Systems and Operations

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, it should be possible to:

  • Remember: identify types of public transit modes and their characteristics.
  • Understand: explain transit capacity, frequency, and service quality.
  • Apply: calculate transit capacity, headways, and fleet requirements.
  • Analyze: evaluate transit service performance and efficiency.
Key vocabulary

Headway: time interval between successive transit vehicles.
Frequency: number of vehicles per unit time (inverse of headway).
Vehicle Capacity: maximum number of passengers per vehicle.
Line Capacity: maximum passengers per hour per direction (pphpd).
Load Factor: ratio of actual passengers to vehicle capacity.
Transit-Oriented Development (TOD): land use planning centered around transit stations.

11.1 Introduction to Public Transportation

Public transportation provides mobility for people without access to private vehicles and supports sustainable urban development. This chapter covers transit modes, operations, and performance metrics.

11.2 Transit Modes and Technologies

Required Bloom level: Remember + Understand

Transit modes range from buses to rail systems. Selection depends on demand, corridor characteristics, and capital costs. Bus rapid transit (BRT), light rail transit (LRT), and heavy rail offer different capacity and speed characteristics.

11.3 Transit Capacity and Operations

Required Bloom level: Apply

Transit capacity depends on vehicle size, frequency, and dwell times at stops. Operations planning involves scheduling, fleet management, and crew assignments to meet service requirements efficiently.

11.4 Transit Performance Evaluation

Required Bloom level: Analyze

Performance metrics include on-time performance, passenger load factors, revenue per mile, and cost efficiency. Service quality affects ridership and public perception of transit.

Sample Questions

Q1 — Bloom's Level: Remember

Question: What is the relationship between headway and frequency?
Answer: Frequency is the inverse of headway. Frequency (vehicles/hour) = 3600 seconds/hour ÷ headway (seconds).

Q2 — Bloom's Level: Apply

Question: A bus route has 10-minute headways using 80-passenger buses. What is the line capacity in passengers per hour per direction (pphpd)?
Answer: Frequency = 60/10 = 6 buses/hour. Line capacity = 6 × 80 = 480 pphpd
