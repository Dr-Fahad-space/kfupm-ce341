Chapter 13

Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS)

Technology Applications in Transportation

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, it should be possible to:

  • Remember: identify ITS technologies and their applications.
  • Understand: explain how ITS improves transportation system performance.
  • Apply: select appropriate ITS solutions for specific problems.
  • Analyze: evaluate ITS benefits and implementation considerations.
Key vocabulary

ITS: Intelligent Transportation Systems applying information technology to transportation.
ATMS: Advanced Traffic Management Systems for real-time traffic control.
ATIS: Advanced Traveler Information Systems providing real-time information.
CCTV: Closed Circuit Television for traffic monitoring.
VMS: Variable Message Signs displaying dynamic information to drivers.
V2X: Vehicle-to-Everything communication technology.

13.1 Introduction to ITS

Intelligent Transportation Systems apply advanced technologies to improve transportation safety, efficiency, and environmental performance. This chapter covers ITS applications in traffic management, traveler information, and vehicle systems.

13.2 Traffic Management Systems

Required Bloom level: Understand

Advanced Traffic Management Systems (ATMS) monitor traffic conditions and implement control strategies. Components include detection, communication, control centers, and field devices like signals and VMS.

13.3 Traveler Information Systems

Required Bloom level: Understand + Apply

Advanced Traveler Information Systems (ATIS) provide real-time traffic and transit information to travelers. Delivery methods include websites, mobile apps, VMS, and in-vehicle systems.

13.4 Connected and Automated Vehicles

Required Bloom level: Understand + Analyze

Connected vehicles communicate with infrastructure and other vehicles (V2X). Automation ranges from driver assistance to fully autonomous operation, with potential impacts on safety, capacity, and travel behavior.

Sample Questions

Q1 — Bloom's Level: Remember

Question: What do ATMS and ATIS stand for in ITS?
Answer: ATMS = Advanced Traffic Management Systems. ATIS = Advanced Traveler Information Systems.

Q2 — Bloom's Level: Apply

Question: A freeway corridor experiences recurring congestion at a bottleneck. What ITS solutions could address this problem?
Answer: Potential solutions include: ramp metering to regulate inflow, VMS to advise travelers of delays, incident detection to identify causes, and travel time information to encourage route diversion.
