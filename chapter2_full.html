Chapter 2

Transportation Agencies in Saudi Arabia — Understanding the System by Function

Transportation engineering is not only about designing roads, railways, airports, and ports. It is also about understanding the public system that plans these networks, sets the rules for using them, funds and delivers projects, enforces safe behavior, and learns from failures. This chapter explains that system in Saudi Arabia in a practical way: by focusing on the functions that any transport system must perform, and then identifying the entities that carry those functions. When you think in terms of functions, the system becomes easier to understand because each agency has a purpose, a set of decisions it controls, and a clear role in the life cycle of transport projects and operations.

A transport system needs multiple functions because transport is high-risk, technically complex, and affects the whole economy. One entity cannot do everything well without creating overload or conflicts of interest. For example, the organization that delivers infrastructure should not be the same organization that independently investigates major accidents, and the organization that enforces traffic laws is not the same organization that regulates transport services. In addition, each mode—roads, public transport, aviation, maritime, rail—has distinct technical standards and operational risks. For these reasons, Saudi Arabia uses specialized entities, each responsible for a specific function.

Learning expectations for this chapter (Bloom’s levels)

By the end of this chapter, it should be possible to:

  • Remember: name key entities and identify their main function.
  • Understand: explain the purpose of each function (policy, regulation, infrastructure responsibility, standards, investigation, enforcement, integration).
  • Apply: for a short scenario, identify which entities should be involved and what each contributes.
Key vocabulary used in this chapter

Policy / strategy: national direction and priorities (what the country wants the transport system to achieve).
Regulation: rule-setting and oversight for a sector (who is allowed to operate, requirements, licensing, compliance).
Infrastructure responsibility: long-term care of networks and terminals (planning programs, maintenance, upgrades, performance).
Technical standards: design and operating requirements that create consistent, predictable outcomes.
Safety oversight: audits, inspections, compliance monitoring, and safety performance management.
Independent safety investigation: root-cause analysis of major accidents to produce recommendations and system learning (not enforcement).
Enforcement: applying laws and penalties to influence user behavior (drivers, vehicle owners, and road users).
Integration: making multiple modes and entities work together as one system (especially in major cities and peak seasons).

1) National policy, strategy, and system leadership

Required Bloom level for this section: Understand

Every country needs a central function that sets national direction: long-term transport priorities, network integration, logistics objectives, and cross-sector coordination. This function ensures that transport aligns with national development goals and that different modes work together rather than compete, duplicate, or leave gaps.

In Saudi Arabia, this leadership role sits with the Ministry of Transport and Logistics Services (وزارة النقل والخدمات اللوجستية).
Website: https://mot.gov.sa/en/home

The ministry’s role is not to manage every project detail directly; it steers the overall system by defining priorities, coordinating national programs, and driving integration across modes so the transport and logistics sector advances as a coherent whole.

What this function does (typical responsibilities):

  • Sets sector direction and high-level priorities.
  • Coordinates cross-mode programs (roads, logistics, aviation, rail, ports) so they align.
  • Enables system-level integration and strategic transformation.

What this function does not do:

  • It is not the day-to-day regulator of individual service providers.
  • It is not traffic enforcement.
  • It is not independent accident investigation.
  • It is not the detailed designer for every road or intersection.
Example

A national goal to improve network quality or logistics performance is set at this level, but the delivery of projects and services is carried by specialized entities in each mode.

2) Regulation and market governance

Required Bloom level for this section: Understand → Apply

A transport system needs rules. Regulation is the function that controls who is allowed to provide transport services, what requirements those services must meet, and how users and operators are protected. Regulation prevents disorder in the market: unlicensed operators, unsafe services, and inconsistent service quality. For engineers, regulation matters because it creates the boundaries within which design and operations must work.

2.1 Transport General Authority (TGA) — land, rail, and maritime regulation

الهيئة العامة للنقل
Website: https://www.tga.gov.sa/en

The Transport General Authority (TGA) is the regulator for land transport, rail transport, and maritime transport as service sectors. This includes licensing and oversight of service providers and setting regulatory frameworks that shape how services operate and how quality and safety are maintained.

What TGA does:

  • Licenses regulated transport activities in land/rail/maritime sectors.
  • Sets compliance requirements and monitors service quality/safety for regulated activities.
  • Issues regulatory frameworks that affect how transport services are provided.

What TGA does not do:

  • It is not traffic enforcement on streets.
  • It is not the owner/operator of the national road network.
  • It is not the independent investigator of major accidents.
Example

If a company wants to legally operate a regulated transport service, the permission-to-operate and requirements function belongs to the regulator, even though vehicles physically operate on roads managed by other entities.

2.2 General Authority of Civil Aviation (GACA) — aviation regulation

الهيئة العامة للطيران المدني
Website: https://gaca.gov.sa/en

The General Authority of Civil Aviation (GACA) regulates the aviation sector. Aviation is treated as a separate regulatory domain because it is highly technical and safety-critical and requires deep expertise and alignment with international aviation standards and practices.

What GACA does:

  • Provides aviation regulations and oversight.
  • Manages licensing and regulatory requirements for aviation activities.
  • Ensures compliance with aviation safety and operational requirements.

What GACA does not do:

  • It does not regulate land/rail/maritime services (that is handled in the land/rail/maritime regulatory domain).
  • It does not enforce road traffic laws.
Example

If an airline or airport activity requires authorization, the rules and approvals are handled under aviation regulation rather than under land transport regulation.

3) Infrastructure governance and long-term infrastructure responsibility (by mode)

Required Bloom level for this section: Understand → Apply

A transport system also needs organizations that take responsibility for infrastructure: networks and terminals that must be planned, developed, maintained, and improved. This function is about assets and long-term performance: condition, capacity, resilience, and reliability. This is where capital programs and maintenance programs live.

3.1 Roads — Roads General Authority (RGA)

الهيئة العامة للطرق
Website: https://www.rga.gov.sa/content/externalportal/en/home.html

In the road sector, the Roads General Authority (RGA) has a sector-level role that supports a unified road system, including consistent practices and performance expectations across the road sector.

What RGA does (road sector role):

  • Supports road-sector governance and unified approaches across the road system.
  • Promotes consistent practices and performance expectations for road infrastructure.

What RGA does not do:

  • It is not traffic enforcement.
  • It is not independent accident investigation.
  • It is not the operator of public transport services.
Example

If a road project must follow unified national road practices, the project is expected to align with road-sector frameworks that make outcomes consistent across regions.

3.2 Ports — Saudi Ports Authority (MAWANI)

الهيئة العامة للموانئ (موانئ)
Website: https://mawani.gov.sa

Ports are complex systems where infrastructure, safety, capacity, and logistics performance must work together. MAWANI leads the development and management of ports within its scope, focusing on port performance and long-term port capability.

What MAWANI does:

  • Develops and manages port infrastructure and port facilities within its scope.
  • Improves port capacity, efficiency, and overall port performance.
  • Acts as a key “owner/client” function for port development programs.

What MAWANI does not do:

  • It does not regulate civil aviation.
  • It does not enforce road traffic laws.
  • It does not replace the national regulator function for transport services.
Example

If a port has congestion, the solution may involve berth capacity, yard layout, gate operations, and terminal organization—issues that belong to port management and development, not road enforcement.

3.3 Rail — Saudi Arabia Railways (SAR)

الخطوط الحديدية السعودية
Website: https://sar.com.sa

Rail requires integrated management because track, rolling stock, signaling, operations, and maintenance must function as one coherent system. Saudi Arabia Railways (SAR) is central to intercity rail infrastructure and operations.

What SAR does:

  • Operates passenger and freight rail services and manages key rail assets within its network.
  • Plans, maintains, and improves rail infrastructure and supporting facilities as part of rail system performance.

What SAR does not do:

  • It does not regulate aviation.
  • It does not enforce road traffic laws.
  • It does not replace the rail-sector regulation function.
Example

If a station needs redesign for passenger flow and safety, SAR is typically the operational client. If the issue is about sector requirements for regulated rail activities, the regulator function is separate from operations.

3.4 Airports — MATARAT Holding ecosystem

شركة مطارات القابضة
Website: https://matarat.com.sa/en-us/Pages/default.aspx

Airport infrastructure and operational management is carried through the aviation ecosystem, including airport operating companies and the transformation platform led by MATARAT Holding (شركة مطارات القابضة).

What MATARAT (ecosystem role) does:

  • Supports transformation and development of airport assets through its ecosystem of airport operating entities.
  • Acts as an owner/client platform for airport development and performance improvement.

What MATARAT does not do:

  • It does not replace the aviation regulator function (GACA).
  • It does not enforce road traffic laws.
Example

A terminal expansion project is typically delivered with the airport operator side as the project client/owner, while aviation compliance requirements come from the aviation regulator.

4) Technical standards and consistent engineering practice

Required Bloom level for this section: Understand → Apply

A transport system needs technical requirements that translate safety and performance expectations into consistent engineering decisions. Technical standards exist because designs must be consistent across projects to produce predictable outcomes and reduce avoidable risk.

In Saudi Arabia, technical requirements are especially visible in the road sector through road-sector frameworks and unified practices supported by the Roads General Authority (الهيئة العامة للطرق).
Website: https://www.rga.gov.sa/content/externalportal/en/home.html

Regulators also embed technical compliance requirements into licensing and oversight processes, particularly in highly technical domains such as aviation.

Example

Two highway projects should not produce very different safety outcomes simply because they are in different regions. Standards and consistent requirements reduce randomness and improve predictability.

5) Safety oversight versus independent safety investigation

Required Bloom level for this section: Understand → Apply

Safety is addressed through more than one function.

Safety oversight exists inside regulators and infrastructure owners. It includes safety requirements, audits, inspections, compliance monitoring, and safety performance management. Oversight is continuous and preventive.

Independent safety investigation exists to learn from major incidents and produce recommendations based on technical findings. This function focuses on system learning rather than penalties.

In Saudi Arabia, independent investigation is carried out by the National Transport Safety Center (NTSC) — المركز الوطني لسلامة النقل.
Website: https://ntsc.gov.sa/en

What NTSC does:

  • Conducts independent investigations of serious transport accidents.
  • Identifies root causes and issues recommendations to prevent recurrence.
  • Supports safety learning across multiple transport modes.

What NTSC does not do:

  • It does not issue traffic tickets or apply penalties.
  • It does not replace regulators’ licensing and compliance roles.
Example

After a major accident, NTSC produces findings and recommendations. Implementation may then require actions by regulators (rules), infrastructure owners (design/maintenance changes), and enforcement entities (behavioral compliance).

6) Traffic enforcement and user-side compliance

Required Bloom level for this section: Remember → Understand → Apply

Transport safety also depends on day-to-day user compliance. This function enforces traffic laws, manages traffic procedures, and applies penalties to influence behavior.

In Saudi Arabia, this function is carried by Traffic (المرور) within the Ministry of Interior (وزارة الداخلية).
Website: https://www.moi.gov.sa/wps/portal/Home/sectors/publicsecurity/traffic

What Traffic (المرور) does:

  • Enforces traffic laws and addresses day-to-day road-user compliance.
  • Supports traffic safety activities and operational traffic management functions within its scope.

What Traffic (المرور) does not do:

  • It does not design road geometry or deliver infrastructure projects.
  • It does not perform independent accident investigations.
Example

If a corridor has frequent speeding and red-light violations, enforcement can change behavior quickly. If the road layout or visibility creates confusion, engineering changes are also required.

7) Local and regional transport integration (cities and special zones)

Required Bloom level for this section: Understand → Apply

Large urban areas and special zones require a function that integrates modes locally: network integration, operational coordination, public transport planning integration, and management of peak demands. This matters most where multi-modal systems must behave as one network and where disruptions have high cost.

7.1 Riyadh — Royal Commission for Riyadh City (RCRC)

الهيئة الملكية لمدينة الرياض
Website: https://www.rcrc.gov.sa/en

This is a city-level integration body associated with Riyadh’s planning and development programs, including transport-related integration initiatives.

Example

Integration at the city level can include aligning bus feeder routes with metro stations, managing corridor priorities, and coordinating across agencies for implementation.

7.2 Makkah and Holy Sites — Royal Commission for Makkah City and Holy Sites & General Center for Transport

الهيئة الملكية لمدينة مكة المكرمة والمشاعر المقدسة – المركز العام للنقل
Website (RCMC main): https://www.rcmc.gov.sa/en-us/
Website (Transport page): https://www.rcmc.gov.sa/makkah-transport

This integration function is especially critical because peak demand periods require exceptional coordination across many entities and modes.

Example

During peak seasons, coordinated shuttle operations, staged movement plans, and managed access are examples of integration work that cannot be achieved by a single mode or single operator acting alone.

A practical method: classify the problem before naming an agency Required Bloom level for this section: Apply

A common mistake is to start with “Which agency?” A more reliable method is:

  1. Identify the function the problem belongs to.
  2. Identify the primary entity responsible for that function.
  3. Identify supporting entities that must coordinate.

Worked example: Crash hotspot on an urban arterial

A crash hotspot is rarely solved by one function alone.

  • Traffic (المرور): contributes by improving compliance and enforcement where behavior is a major driver.
  • Road authority / project owner (road infrastructure side): contributes by implementing engineering changes (geometry, control, access management, signing/marking, lighting, pedestrian treatments).
  • NTSC (المركز الوطني لسلامة النقل): contributes when crashes are severe or major by analyzing root causes and issuing recommendations for system-level learning.

The key idea is recognizing how functions combine to produce a complete solution.

Summary: A simple way to remember the system

Required Bloom level for this section: Remember → Understand

The transportation system in Saudi Arabia becomes logical when you identify the function first, then the entity:

  • National direction: Ministry of Transport and Logistics Services (وزارة النقل والخدمات اللوجستية) — https://mot.gov.sa/en/home
  • Regulation (land/rail/maritime): Transport General Authority (الهيئة العامة للنقل) — https://www.tga.gov.sa/en
  • Regulation (aviation): General Authority of Civil Aviation (الهيئة العامة للطيران المدني) — https://gaca.gov.sa/en
  • Road-sector governance and unified practices: Roads General Authority (الهيئة العامة للطرق) — https://www.rga.gov.sa/content/externalportal/en/home.html
  • Ports development and management: MAWANI (الهيئة العامة للموانئ / موانئ) — https://mawani.gov.sa
  • Rail operations and key rail assets: SAR (الخطوط الحديدية السعودية) — https://sar.com.sa
  • Airport operator ecosystem / transformation platform: MATARAT (شركة مطارات القابضة) — https://matarat.com.sa
  • Independent safety investigation: NTSC (المركز الوطني لسلامة النقل) — https://ntsc.gov.sa/en
  • Traffic enforcement: Traffic (المرور) within Ministry of Interior (وزارة الداخلية) — https://www.moi.gov.sa/wps/portal/Home/sectors/publicsecurity/traffic
  • City-level integration: RCRC (الهيئة الملكية لمدينة الرياض) — https://www.rcrc.gov.sa/en and RCMC General Center for Transport (المركز العام للنقل) — https://www.rcmc.gov.sa/makkah-transport

When these functions are understood clearly, transportation governance becomes a coherent system that supports planning, design, delivery, operation, and safety—rather than a list of names to memorize.

Sample Questions and Answers (for practice)

Answer each question in one to two sentences. Bloom’s taxonomy level is shown for self-assessment.

Q1 — Bloom’s Level: Understand

Question: Why is a function-based approach useful for understanding how transportation agencies are organized in Saudi Arabia?
Answer: Because focusing on functions clarifies what needs to be done first and helps identify the correct responsible entity without confusion.

Q2 — Bloom’s Level: Understand

Question: Why are transportation responsibilities divided among multiple entities rather than handled by a single organization?
Answer: Because transportation is complex and high-risk; dividing responsibilities reduces conflicts of interest and allows specialization.

Q3 — Bloom’s Level: Remember

Question: Which entity provides national leadership, policy direction, and coordination for the transport and logistics sector in Saudi Arabia?
Answer: The Ministry of Transport and Logistics Services (وزارة النقل والخدمات اللوجستية).

Q4 — Bloom’s Level: Understand

Question: In one sentence, explain what regulation means in the context of transportation systems.
Answer: Regulation is the function of setting and enforcing rules that determine who may provide transport services and under what conditions.

Q5 — Bloom’s Level: Apply

Question: A company wants to legally operate a new commercial land transport service. Which entity should it deal with, and why?
Answer: The Transport General Authority (الهيئة العامة للنقل), because it regulates land transport services and operator requirements.

Q6 — Bloom’s Level: Understand

Question: What is meant by infrastructure responsibility in a transportation system?
Answer: It refers to planning, maintaining, and improving transport networks to ensure long-term performance and reliability.

Q7 — Bloom’s Level: Apply

Question: You are working on a road project that must follow unified national road practices. Which entity supports this role, and how does it affect the project?
Answer: The Roads General Authority (الهيئة العامة للطرق); it influences projects by promoting unified frameworks and consistent road-sector practices.

Q8 — Bloom’s Level: Understand

Question: Why are technical standards important in transportation engineering projects?
Answer: They ensure consistent design, predictable performance, and acceptable safety levels across different projects and locations.

Q9 — Bloom’s Level: Understand

Question: Explain the difference between traffic enforcement and independent safety investigation.
Answer: Traffic enforcement focuses on controlling user behavior through laws and penalties, while independent investigation analyzes major accidents to identify causes and prevent recurrence.

Q10 — Bloom’s Level: Apply

Question: A serious transport accident occurs and the goal is to determine root causes and issue safety recommendations, not penalties. Which entity is responsible?
Answer: The National Transport Safety Center (المركز الوطني لسلامة النقل).

Q11 — Bloom’s Level: Remember

Question: Which entity is responsible for traffic enforcement and managing driver behavior on roads in Saudi Arabia?
Answer: Traffic (المرور) within the Ministry of Interior (وزارة الداخلية).

Q12 — Bloom’s Level: Apply

Question: Improving enforcement alone is proposed as a solution to frequent crashes. Identify one additional role that must be involved and explain why.
Answer: Infrastructure improvement must be involved because crashes can result from road geometry or layout that enforcement alone cannot correct.

Q13 — Bloom’s Level: Understand

Question: Why is aviation regulated separately from roads and land transport in Saudi Arabia?
Answer: Because aviation is highly technical and safety-critical and requires deep expertise and alignment with international aviation standards.

Q14 — Bloom’s Level: Apply

Question: A crash hotspot exists on an urban road. List three entities that could contribute to solving the problem and briefly state the role of each.
Answer: Traffic (المرور) improves compliance; the road authority or project owner improves road design and operations; the National Transport Safety Center (المركز الوطني لسلامة النقل) analyzes serious crashes and recommends system improvements.
