Chapter 3: Characteristics of Road Users, Vehicles, and Roads

3.1 Perception-Reaction Time (PRT)

Distance during PRT:

$$d = u \times t$$

Where: \(d\) = distance (m), \(u\) = speed (m/s), \(t\) = PRT (2.5 s design value)

Speed conversion:

$$u = V \times \frac{1000}{3600} = V \times 0.278$$

Where \(V\) = speed in km/h

3.2 Visual Acuity

$$\frac{d_1}{d_2} = \frac{6/X_1}{6/X_2}$$

If sign readable at distance \(d\) for 6/6 acuity: 6/4 acuity → \(d \times 1.5\); 6/10 acuity → \(d \times 0.6\)

3.3 Pedestrian Walking Speed

Pedestrian clearance time:

$$t = \frac{\text{Crossing distance}}{\text{Walking speed}}$$
Population Design Walking Speed
General (≤20% elderly) 1.22 m/s
Elderly-predominant (>20%) 0.88 m/s

3.4 Vehicle Braking Distance

Braking distance:

$$D_b = \frac{u^2}{254\left(\frac{a}{g} \pm G\right)}$$

Where: \(u\) = speed (km/h), \(a/g = 0.35\) (AASHTO), \(G\) = grade (decimal)
Use + for uphill (gravity assists braking), − for downhill (gravity opposes braking)

3.5 Stopping Sight Distance (SSD)

$$\text{SSD} = 0.278 \cdot u \cdot t + \frac{u^2}{254\left(\frac{a}{g} \pm G\right)}$$

Where: \(u\) = speed (km/h), \(t\) = PRT (2.5 s), \(a/g = 0.35\), \(G\) = grade

On level roads (G = 0):

$$\text{SSD} = 0.278ut + \frac{u^2}{88.9}$$

3.6 Deceleration to Lower Speed

$$D = \frac{u_1^2 - u_2^2}{254\left(\frac{a}{g} \pm G\right)}$$

Where: \(u_1\) = initial speed, \(u_2\) = final speed (km/h)

3.7 Horizontal Curve - Minimum Radius

$$R = \frac{u^2}{127(e + f_s)}$$

Where: \(u\) = speed (km/h), \(e\) = superelevation (max 0.10 rural), \(f_s\) = side-friction factor

Chapter 4: Geometric Design — Vertical Alignment

4.1 Stationing

$$\text{Sta.}_{\text{BVC}} = \text{Sta.}_{\text{PVI}} - \frac{L}{2}$$ $$\text{Sta.}_{\text{EVC}} = \text{Sta.}_{\text{PVI}} + \frac{L}{2}$$

4.2 Percent Grade

$$G = \frac{\Delta\text{Elevation}}{\Delta\text{Horizontal Distance}} \times 100$$

4.3 Algebraic Difference in Grades

$$A = |G_1 - G_2|$$

Crest curve: \(G_1 > G_2\) | Sag curve: \(G_1 < G_2\)

4.4 Parabolic Vertical Curve Equation

General form:

$$y = ax^2 + bx + c$$

\(c\) = Elevation at BVC (m)
\(b = G_1/100\) = initial tangent grade (m/m)
\(a = \dfrac{G_2 - G_1}{200L}\) = curvature coefficient
    • \(a < 0\): crest curve (opens downward)
    • \(a > 0\): sag curve (opens upward)

Slope at any point:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = 2ax + b$$

4.5 K-Value

$$K = \frac{L}{A}$$

\(K\) = horizontal distance (m) required for 1% change in grade

4.6 BVC and EVC Elevations

$$\text{Elev}_{\text{BVC}} = \text{Elev}_{\text{PVI}} - G_1 \times \frac{L/2}{100}$$ $$\text{Elev}_{\text{EVC}} = \text{Elev}_{\text{PVI}} + G_2 \times \frac{L/2}{100}$$

4.7 Elevation at Any Point

Method 1: Direct Parabolic Equation

$$y = ax^2 + bx + c$$

Method 2: Tangent-Plus-Offset

Step 1 - Tangent elevation:

$$\text{Tangent Elev} = \text{Elev}_{\text{BVC}} + G_1 \times \frac{x}{100}$$

Step 2 - Parabolic offset:

$$Y = \frac{A}{200L} \times x^2$$

Step 3 - Apply offset:

$$\text{Curve Elev} = \text{Tangent Elev} \mp Y$$

Use − for crest (curve below tangent), + for sag (curve above tangent)
